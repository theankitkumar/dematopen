What is a Demat Account?

A Demat Account is a digital account that holds your shares and securities in an electronic format.

Show More

A Demat Account, short for Dematerialized Account, allows investors to hold their shares and securities electronically instead of physical certificates. This makes it easier to manage and trade securities. Essentially, it's like a bank account but for your investments. Instead of keeping physical share certificates, all your investments are stored digitally in your Demat Account. This not only makes it convenient but also reduces the risk of loss or damage to physical certificates.

Difference Between Trading and Demat Account

Understand the key differences between a Trading Account and a Demat Account.

Show More

A Trading Account is used for buying and selling securities, while a Demat Account is used to hold those securities electronically. Both are essential for trading in the stock market. Think of it like this: the Trading Account is your tool for making transactions (buying and selling), while the Demat Account is your storage for the securities you own. You need both to participate in the stock market effectively.

Behind the Scenes Process in Market

Learn about the behind-the-scenes processes that occur in the market.

Show More

The market involves various behind-the-scenes processes such as order matching, settlement, and clearing. These processes ensure smooth and secure trading. When you place an order to buy or sell shares, these processes ensure that the transaction is completed accurately and securely. Order matching ensures that your buy or sell order is matched with a corresponding sell or buy order. Settlement involves the actual transfer of shares and funds between the buyer and seller. Clearing is the process that ensures all transactions are properly accounted for and settled.
